Chicken Dum Biryani

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 2 cardamom pods, 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • Fried onions for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, mint leaves, coriander leaves, and salt. Set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, heat ghee over medium heat. Add the whole spices and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add marinated chicken and cook until it's half done.
  7. Layer soaked rice evenly over the chicken mixture. Pour water gently over the rice.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the chicken is tender.
  9. Once done, turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.
  10. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork, garnish with fried onions, and serve hot with raita or salad.
