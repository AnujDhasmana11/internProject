Vegetable Dum Biryani Recipe:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 2 cardamom pods, 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • Fried onions for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee over medium heat. Add the whole spices and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Now, add mixed vegetables, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  7. Lower the heat and add yogurt. Mix until well combined.
  8. Layer soaked rice evenly over the vegetable mixture. Pour water gently over the rice.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  10. Once done, turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.
  11. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork, garnish with fried onions, and serve hot with raita or salad.
